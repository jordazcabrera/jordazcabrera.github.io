Meet-Up Report - Thiknful Online Technology

The information in this page is for my CNIT-132 class assignment at City Collegue of San Francisco. The assignment was to participate in one meeting or workshop related to web development. In 1/29/19, I joined a group called Learn to Code | Thinkful San Francisco through MeetUp.

MeetUp is a global platform where people with similar interest come together to create, share, find and attend events. It is easy to sing up and most importantly it is free! Some of the things I learned by joining a group is that you definitely learn new things, find the support you need, and help you get out of your comfort zone.

Thinkful San Francisco is a new type of online school that can help you build a career in web development and data science by offering different bootcamps in frontend and backend development.

Thinkful's ideology is that you have the potential to become a professional at your own terms and convinience with the support of their network of industry experts, hiring partners, and online platform.

With its "Tuition Refund" policy, Thinkful guarantess full refund of your tuition if you are not offered a position within six months of graduation. Thinkful workshops are offered at The Vault , located at 415 Jackson St, San Francisco, CA 94111.

What does Thinkful Offer?

  • 1-on-1 way to learn through online platform.
  • Most pupular free tutorials
  • Tuition reimbursement or pay nothing until you land a job.
  • Receive support of a mentor, career coach, and program manager to guide you to the next level in your career.
  • Series of full-time, part-time, and flexible courses:
    • Engineering Immmersion
    • Full Stack Flex
    • Engineering Nights and Weekends
    • Data Science Flexible
    • Data Analytics Flexible
    • product Design Flexible

Learn to Code - Group

This is the group I joined and it offered multiple workshops pretty much every day. In the events section of the group page you can find a vast variety of courses. I went to more than one meetings but the two I enjoyed the most were Build Your Own Website and Front-End.

These are some of the things I learned in both meetings:

  • How the Web works
  • Client vs Server
  • Front Developers vs Backend Developers
  • HTML Structure
  • HTML: tags, elements, and attributes.
  • CSS: selectors, properties, and values.
  • Use of CodePen , a social development environment for front-end designers and developers.

In every meeting, you get access to two-week free trial. You also do a video conference with a Thinkful Project Manager who gives you information about how Thinkful platform and programs work.
